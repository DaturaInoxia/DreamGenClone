## Template Usage Guidelines

### Before Creating a New Theme:

1. **Identify the core scenario type:** What is the fundamental narrative pattern?
2. **Determine if it's a variant:** Does it build on an existing theme? If so, mark as "Variant of" and clearly describe differences
3. **Map character roles:** Who are the key participants? What are their relationships?
4. **Identify stat patterns:** What character states indicate this scenario is appropriate?
5. **Define the narrative arc:** Build-up → Committed → Approaching → Climax → Reset

### Keywords Section Best Practices:

- Use 5-7 keyword categories
- 5-10 keywords per category
- Mix common words and scenario-specific terms
- Include both explicit and implicit indicators (e.g., "nervous" and "pulse" both indicate tension)
- Include actions, emotions, settings, and objects

### Stat Affinities Best Practices:

- Consider the emotional journey of each character
- Use +1 to +3 for significant positive effects
- Use -1 to -3 for significant negative effects
- Include rationale for each stat - this helps explain the scenario dynamics
- Total stat changes should reflect the overall emotional tone

### Fit Logic Best Practices:

- Define 4-5 key stat thresholds per character
- Thresholds should be 30-80 range (avoid extreme 0/100 unless critical)
- Explain what each threshold represents narratively
- Weight the fit formula based on most important indicators
- Ensure formula weights sum to 1.0

### Scenario Guidance Best Practices:

- Each phase should build toward the next
- Build-Up: Setup, tension creation, establishing possibility
- Committed: Narrative direction set, focused development
- Approaching: Intensity increases, decision points emerge
- Climax: Peak moment, scenario-defining content
- Reset: Aftermath, processing, transition to next scenario

### AI Generation Notes Best Practices:

- Be specific about what makes THIS scenario unique
- Contrast with related themes to prevent overlap
- Give clear "what to avoid" guidelines
- Include internal monologue guidance when appropriate
- Distinguish between what characters feel vs. what they show

## Example Character Role Labels

Use consistent terminology across themes:

- **Transgressor:** Character engaging in illicit/secret behavior
- **Primary Partner:** The person in primary relationship with transgressor
- **Observing Partner:** Partner who discovers or watches
- **Consenting Partner:** Partner who knows and agrees to the scenario
- **Secondary Participant:** The other person involved in the scenario
- **Observer:** Character watching without being involved
- **Initiator:** Character who initiates the scenario or encounter

## Example Stat Threshold Ranges

Use these as guidelines when setting fit logic thresholds:

| Stat | Low (≤40) | Moderate (40-60) | High (≥70) | Extreme (≥85) |
|------|-----------|------------------|------------|---------------|
| **Tension** | Relaxed, calm | Normal baseline | Stressed, anxious | Panicking, overwhelmed |
| **Desire** | Low motivation | Normal interest | Strong attraction | Urgent, overwhelming |
| **Restraint** | Impulsive, unrestrained | Normal self-control | Holding back, tense | Extreme control, frozen |
