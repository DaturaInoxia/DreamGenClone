# Theme Definition: Infidelity with Public Discovery

## Description

A scenario where infidelity occurs while the partner is physically present—either nearby or within conversational range—and the partner discovers the transgression or already knows about it. The transgression may happen in adjacent spaces (briefly separated at a party) OR concealed in plain sight (under a table, behind a counter, or otherwise hidden from the waist down). Unlike the base variant where the partner is unaware, here the partner's knowledge creates additional tension: they may be told afterward, discover during the act, or already knew and chose not to intervene. After the encounter, the transgressor may or may not be aware that their partner knows.

**Key Difference from Base Variant:**
- **Infidelity-Public-Facade (base):** Husband is unaware, wife maintains secret facade
- **Infidelity-Public-Discovery (this variant):** Husband knows or discovers, creating knowledge-based tension

**Discovery/Knowledge Variants:**
- **Discovery during act:** Husband discovers while it's happening (sees, hears, or finds evidence)
- **Told afterward:** Husband is informed after the encounter concludes
- **Pre-existing knowledge:** Husband already knew before this encounter, creating sustained tension

## Scenario Guidance

### Build-Up Phase

**Character Action Suggestions:**
- Partner (husband) begins with suspicion or notices unusual behavior
- Subtle investigation or following partner to confirm suspicions
- Discovery moment: seeing, hearing, or finding evidence of transgression
- Internal processing of betrayal, anger, shock
- Decision about when/how to confront or reveal knowledge

**Atmosphere:**
- Suspenseful discovery sequence
- Shock and emotional turmoil in the discovering partner
- Public/social setting constrains immediate reaction (no screaming, no scene)
- Undercurrent of betrayal and broken trust

### Committed Phase

**Narrative Focus:**
- The discovery and its impact on the discovering partner
- The decision about confrontation timing (immediate vs. delayed)
- The tension of knowing while transgression continues or concludes
- The emotional fallout and processing of betrayal
- How the discovery affects the immediate social interaction

**Character Development:**
- Discovering partner: Shock, anger, hurt, confusion, emotional turmoil
- Transgressor: Engaged in encounter, potential awareness or complete oblivion
- The other party: Engaged in encounter, may be collateral in the fallout

### Approaching Phase

**Heightened Elements:**
- Discovering partner's internal conflict: rage vs. restraint
- Physical reactions to discovery (shaking, nausea, rage, numbness)
- Decision moment: confront now? Wait until later? Pretend nothing happened?
- The encounter concluding - what happens next in the social setting?
- The transgressor's potential intuition that something is wrong

**Internal Monologue/Conflict:**
- How could you do this?
- I want to scream at you right now
- I can't make a scene here
- I need to know everything
- I can't believe this is happening
- Does she know I know?

### Climax Phase

**Scenario-Specific Context:**
- The emotional climax is the confrontation or revelation of knowledge
- The encounter itself is secondary - the focus is on the betrayal and its discovery
- The moment knowledge is shared or confrontation occurs
- The transgressor's reaction to discovery (shock, guilt, denial, defensiveness)

**Character Perspectives:**
- Discovering Partner: Intense betrayal, anger, hurt, shock. The climax is revealing knowledge and confronting the transgression.
- Transgressor: Shock at discovery (if unaware), or guilt and defensiveness (if caught). May also feel relief that secret is out.
- Other Party: Caught in the middle, may withdraw or be confronted

**Confrontation Variations:**
1. **Immediate confrontation:** Partner reveals knowledge immediately, possibly in semi-private setting
2. **Delayed confrontation:** Partner waits until after social event, then reveals knowledge at home
3. **Silent knowledge:** Partner never reveals knowledge openly, carries it forward (may evolve to other scenarios)
4. **Public confrontation:** Partner creates scene despite constraints (highest tension)

### Reset Phase

**Post-Climax Elements:**
- Relationship fundamentally altered by discovery
- Trust must be rebuilt or relationship ends
- Potential for forgiveness, revenge, or relationship dissolution
- Future scenarios may involve reconciliation, separation, or revenge infidelity
- The knowledge cannot be unknown - relationship changes permanently
